A simple experiment to make a natural moisturizing cream using safe, natural ingredients.  
This project shows how emulsification helps mix oil and water to form a smooth, hydrating cream.

### Objective
To understand how oil and water can be combined into a stable emulsion using an emulsifier.

---

###  Ingredients
- Distilled water (60 ml)  
- Aloe vera gel (10 ml)  
- Sweet almond oil (20 ml)  
- Shea butter (10 g)  
- Emulsifying wax (5 g)  
- Vitamin E oil (3 drops)  
- Essential oil (optional)  
- Preservative (optional)

---

###  Equipment
- 2 heat-resistant glass bowls  
- 1 pot (for double boiler)  
- Measuring spoons or scale  
- Whisk or spoon  
- Clean jar  

---
## 2. Formula for 100 g of Cream

The formulation consists of three phases: an oil phase, a water phase, and a cool-down phase.

For the oil phase, you will need 10 grams of sweet almond oil as an emollient, 5 grams of shea butter to provide deep moisturization, 4 grams of emulsifying wax to help bind the oil and water together, and 2 grams of cetyl alcohol to thicken and stabilize the emulsion.

For the water phase, use 68 grams of distilled water as the main solvent, 3 grams of glycerin as a humectant to draw moisture into the skin, and 1 gram of panthenol (vitamin B5) to soothe and hydrate.

Finally, in the cool-down phase, include 1 gram of preservative such as phenoxyethanol or Optiphen to prevent microbial growth, 0.5 grams of vitamin E as an antioxidant to protect the oils from oxidation, and 0.5 grams of essential oil (optional) for fragrance or additional skin benefits.

The total formula yields 100 grams of cream.
